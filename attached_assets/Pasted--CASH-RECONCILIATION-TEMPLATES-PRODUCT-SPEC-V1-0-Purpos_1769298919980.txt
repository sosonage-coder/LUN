Purpose

Cash reconciliation templates define the standardized structure used to reconcile monetary cash accounts on the balance sheet.

They ensure:

Consistent treatment of FX revaluation

Clear separation of operational reconciling items

Audit-readable explanations of balance movements

Reusability across entities, periods, and bank accounts

Templates define structure and required sections, not data.

Scope (V1)

This spec covers:

Template types

Required sections

Data expectations

FX treatment rules

It does not cover:

Bank feeds

Auto-matching

Posting logic

Reporting currency frameworks

1Ô∏è‚É£ Template Classification

Cash templates apply only to monetary accounts.

Eligibility Rules

A cash template may be used when:

The account represents cash or cash equivalents

The balance is subject to period-end FX revaluation

The account is reconciled against external bank balances

Templates are account-agnostic, but account-appropriate.

3Ô∏è‚É£ Common Structure (Applies to All Cash Templates)

All cash templates share the same core sections, regardless of variant.

A. Template Metadata (System-Defined)

Each template includes:

Template name

Account classification: Monetary

FX applicable: Yes

Version

Template type: System

Eligible account types: Cash / Cash equivalents

B. Entity & Period Context

Each reconciliation instantiated from the template must specify:

Entity

Period

Reporting currency (for reconciliation display)

C. Bank Account Section

Required fields:

Bank account identifier

Currency (selected from company currency list)

Period-end bank balance

Behavior:

Currency selection is mandatory

FX rate auto-populates based on selected currency

FX rate is visible and editable

D. FX Revaluation Section (Mandatory)

Purpose:
Explain currency-driven balance movements separate from operational activity.

Requirements:

Dedicated FX revaluation section

FX impact shown as a standalone component

FX never embedded within reconciling items

FX section may include:

Auto-calculated FX difference

Manual FX adjustment (if required)

E. Reconciling Items Section (Mandatory)

Reconciling items must be split into two fixed sub-sections:

1. Items in Bank, Not in GL

Examples:

Deposits in transit

Bank fees

Interest

Timing differences

2. Items in GL, Not in Bank

Examples:

Outstanding cheques

Pending payments

Recording errors

Each reconciling item:

Appears as a separate line

Supports attachments

Supports tagging

Has an open / cleared status

F. Summary & Tie-Out Section

Each template must display a visible reconciliation equation:

Total Bank Balance(s)
¬± Reconciling Items
¬± FX Revaluation
= Trial Balance Cash


The net difference must be zero to certify.
